History of Manga

Explore the rich and fascinating evolution of manga from its origins to the global phenomenon it is today.

What is Manga?

Manga (漫画) is a Japanese term that literally means "whimsical pictures" or "casual sketches." Today, it refers to Japanese comic books and graphic novels that have become a major influence on global popular culture. From action-packed adventures to heartfelt romances, manga covers every imaginable genre and appeals to readers of all ages.

Pre-1900s

Ancient Origins

The roots of manga can be traced back to Japanese art forms like scroll paintings and woodblock prints. Artists explored sequential storytelling through visual narratives, laying the foundation for modern manga.

Early 1900s

Birth of Modern Manga

During the early 20th century, manga began to develop its modern form. Influenced by Western comics and Japanese traditions, artists started creating serialized stories in newspapers and magazines.

1945-1960s

Post-War Boom

After World War II, manga exploded in popularity as an affordable form of entertainment. This era saw the creation of iconic works that defined genres like shōnen and shōjo manga. Authors like Osamu Tezuka revolutionized the medium with their innovative storytelling.

1970s-1980s

Golden Age of Manga

This period marked the golden age of manga, with titles like "Dragon Ball," "Akira," and "Sailor Moon" becoming household names. Magazine circulation reached unprecedented levels, and manga became deeply embedded in Japanese culture.

1990s

Anime & Adaptation Boom

Many manga series were adapted into anime, introducing them to international audiences. The 1990s saw tremendous growth in both the manga industry and its connection to digital media and entertainment.

2000s

Global Expansion

Manga began gaining significant traction outside of Japan. English translations became more common, and international publishers started investing in manga distribution. The global manga market began to flourish.

2010s-2020s

Digital Revolution

The rise of digital platforms and manga reading apps transformed how people consume manga. Online serialization, webtoons, and digital distribution became mainstream. Manga achieved unprecedented global recognition and revenue.

Today

A Global Phenomenon

Manga is now a multi-billion dollar industry with a dedicated global fanbase. Platforms like DOKUSHO-ZUKI connect readers worldwide, communities celebrate their favorite series, and manga continues to influence entertainment across all media. The future of manga looks brighter than ever.
